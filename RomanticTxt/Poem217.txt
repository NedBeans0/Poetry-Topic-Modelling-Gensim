as hermes once took to his feathers light when lulled argus baffled swoond and slept so on a delphic reed my idle spright so playd so charmd so conquerd so bereft the dragonworld of all its hundred eyes and seeing it asleep so fled away not to pure ida with its snowcold skies nor unto tempe where jove grievd that day but to that second circle of sad hell where in the gust the whirlwind and the flaw of rain and hailstones lovers need not tell their sorrowspale were the sweet lips i saw pale were the lips i kissd and fair the form i floated with about that melancholy storm
