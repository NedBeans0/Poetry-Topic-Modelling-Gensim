and what is love it is a doll dressd up for idleness to cosset nurse and dandle a thing of soft misnomers so divine that silly youth doth think to make itself divine by loving and so goes on yawning and doting a whole summer long till misss comb is made a pearl tiara and common wellingtons turn romeo boots then cleopatra lives at number seven and antony resides in brunswick square fools if some passions high have warmd the world if queens and soldiers have playd deep for hearts it is no reason why such agonies should be more common than the growth of weeds fools make me whole again that weighty pearl the queen of egypt melted and ill say that ye may love in spite of beaver hats
