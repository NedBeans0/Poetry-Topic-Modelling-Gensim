from low to high doth dissolution climb and sink from high to low along a scale of awful notes whose concord shall not fail a musical but melancholy chime which they can hear who meddle not with crime nor avarice nor overanxious care truth fails not but her outward forms that bear the longest date do melt like frosty rime that in the morning whitened hill and plain and is no more drop like the tower sublime of yesterday which royally did wear his crown of weeds but could not even sustain some casual shout that broke the silent air or the unimaginable touch of time
