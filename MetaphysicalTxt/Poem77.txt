come and let us live my deare let us love and never feare what the sowrest fathers say brightest sol that dies to day lives againe as blithe to morrow but if we darke sons of sorrow set o then how long a night shuts the eyes of our short light then let amorous kisses dwell on our lips begin and tell a thousand and a hundred score an hundred and a thousand more till another thousand smother that and that wipe of another thus at last when we have numbred many a thousand many a hundred weel confound the reckoning quite and lose our selves in wild delight while our joyes so multiply as shall mocke the envious eye
